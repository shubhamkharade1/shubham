Importance of Yoga!

Yoga is an ancient practice that originated in India and encompasses a range of physical, mental, and spiritual disciplines. It is a holistic approach to overall well-being and self-realization. While yoga is commonly associated with physical postures (asanas), it encompasses much more.

Yoga combines physical movement, breath control (pranayama), meditation, ethical principles, and philosophical teachings to promote harmony between the body, mind, and spirit. It seeks to cultivate physical strength, flexibility, and balance, while also calming the mind, reducing stress, and promoting inner peace.

Yoga is not just a form of exercise but a lifestyle that encourages self-awareness, mindfulness, and the development of positive qualities such as compassion, patience, and gratitude. It can be practiced by people of all ages and fitness levels, and there are various styles of yoga, including Hatha, Vinyasa, Ashtanga, Bikram, and more, each with its own emphasis and approach.

YOGA POSES

Mountain Pose (Tadasana):


This foundational pose improves posture, balance, and promotes a sense of grounding and stability.

Benefits: Mountain Pose improves your postural and body awareness. It helps you find alignment by asking you to stack your shoulders, hips, knees, and ankles. And it can counter the effects of prolonged sitting at a computer by reminding you what it feels like to release your shoulders away from your ears.



Downward Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana):


This pose stretches and strengthens the entire body, especially the arms, shoulders, hamstrings, and calves. It also helps relieve stress and rejuvenates the mind.

Benefits: Down Dog Pose strengthens your wrists, arms, and shoulders; it stretches your wrists, hamstrings, and back. Because the posture lengthens your spine, it counteracts the effects of prolonged sitting and improves posture.



Tree Pose (Vrikshasana):

This balancing pose improves focus, concentration, and strengthens the legs. It also enhances stability and promotes a sense of calm.

Benefits: Tree Pose is a strengthening posture that can help build confidence. This pose can improve your posture and counteract the effects of prolonged sitting. On your standing leg, this pose strengthens your thigh, buttock (glute), and ankle. On your lifted leg, this pose gently stretches your entire thigh and buttocks.



Triangle Pose (Trikonasana):

This balancing pose improves focus, concentration, and strengthens the legs. It also enhances stability and promotes a sense of calm.

Benefits: Extended Triangle Pose improves balance, posture, and body awareness. It counteracts the effects of prolonged sitting. Strengthens your thighs, hips, core, back, and side body on the bottom side (including the abdominal obliques)



Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II):

This pose strengthens the legs, opens the hips and chest, and improves stamina. It promotes a feeling of strength, confidence, and grounding.

Benefits: Hamstrings are stretched and opened to the maximum. Athletes gain with the expansion of the hamstrings making it strong. Stretches the knees, ankles and the foot. Acts as a great way to shape the body with the alignment
